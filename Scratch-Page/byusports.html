BYU Football

BYU Football has one of the proudest traditions in all of college football. From the legendary coaching of LaVell Edwards to the electric atmosphere of LaVell Edwards Stadium, the Cougars have always competed at a high level. Now in the Big 12, BYU football is playing on the biggest stage it has ever seen.

BYU Football Highlights

  1. National Championship (1984)
    • Finished the season 13-0
    • Coached by LaVell Edwards
    • QB Robbie Bosco was outstanding all year
  2. Heisman Trophy Winners
    • Ty Detmer, 1990
    • Only BYU player to win the Heisman
    • Set numerous NCAA passing records
  3. Big 12 Era (2023-Present)
    • First Power 5 conference in program history
    • Playing against Texas, Oklahoma, Kansas State and more
    • Recruiting at an all-time high level

BYU Basketball

The Marriott Center is one of the loudest arenas in college basketball when the Cougars are on a run. BYU Basketball has a long history of producing elite guards and making deep NCAA Tournament runs. With passionate fans and a program that consistently develops top talent, Cougar basketball is must-watch entertainment from November through March.

Why Cougar Basketball Stands Out

  1. The Marriott Center Experience
    • Capacity of over 18,000 fans
    • One of the loudest arenas in the country on big game nights
    • Known for incredible student section energy
  2. Tournament Success
    • Multiple Sweet 16 appearances
    • Consistently competes in March Madness
    • Strong history of guard play and team basketball

NIL and BYU Athletics

Name, Image, and Likeness (NIL) has completely transformed college athletics since it was introduced in 2021. BYU athletes now have the ability to build personal brands, sign endorsement deals, and earn income while still competing for the Cougars. NIL has changed everything from recruiting to team dynamics, and BYU has embraced it as part of the modern college sports landscape.

Key NIL Facts

  1. What NIL Means for Athletes
    • Athletes can sign endorsement deals with local and national brands
    • Social media presence has become a major factor in NIL value
    • Athletes retain their eligibility while earning income
  2. Impact on BYU Recruiting
    • BYU can now compete financially with larger programs
    • Collective groups help pool NIL money for top recruits
    • Utah market offers strong local business partnerships
